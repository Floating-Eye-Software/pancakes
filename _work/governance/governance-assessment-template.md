# Purpose

The Governance Assessment is the final governance review artifact.

It evaluates governance evidence produced through:

* Standards Applicability Profiles (SAPs),
* Standards Assessments,
* Safety Cases,
* Ethics Cases,
* Privacy Reviews,
* Threat Models,
* Stewardship Reviews,

and records the resulting governance decision.

The Governance Assessment does not replace those artifacts.

Its purpose is to evaluate them collectively and determine:

* whether the project may proceed,
* under what conditions,
* what governance obligations remain,
* what reassessment triggers exist.

# Strategic Principle

The purpose of governance assessment is not to maximize compliance activity.

The purpose is to determine whether a project can proceed in a manner consistent with:

* dignity,
* autonomy,
* privacy,
* stewardship,
* reciprocity,
* community participation,
* human flourishing,
* and responsible innovation.

The Governance Assessment records the judgment that connects governance principles to implementation decisions.
