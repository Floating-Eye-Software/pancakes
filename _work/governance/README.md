# Pancakes Governance

## Purpose

This directory contains the governance, ethics, stewardship, safety, privacy, and assurance frameworks used throughout the Pancakes, Pitchfork, Red Witch, Nexus, and related ecosystem projects.

The purpose of governance within Pancakes is not merely compliance.

The purpose is to help ensure that systems remain:

* humane,
* privacy-preserving,
* stewardable,
* auditable,
* accountable,
* non-exploitative,
* and aligned with human flourishing.

Governance exists to support people, households, communities, and future generations—not to create unnecessary bureaucracy.

---

# Governance Philosophy

The Pancakes ecosystem is built around several core principles:

* Human dignity exceeds computational utility.
* Privacy is a stewardship obligation.
* Communities should be governable.
* Households should remain sovereign.
* Technology should support flourishing rather than extraction.
* Safety should be evaluated through human outcomes.
* Standards are tools, not goals.
* Incentives require ethical review.
* Stewardship is as important as rights.
* Systems should remain adaptable as society learns.

The ecosystem assumes that future societies will discover both:

* new forms of flourishing,
* and new forms of harm.

Governance must therefore remain capable of learning.

---

# Governance Stack

The governance architecture is organized into layers.

```text
Charter of Rights and Freedoms
            ↓
Human Flourishing Framework
            ↓
Common Good Model
            ↓
Stewardship Model
            ↓
Standards Model
            ↓
Safety Cases Framework
            ↓
Project Assessments
            ↓
Implementation Decisions
```

Each layer answers a different question.

---

# Layer 1: Charter of Rights and Freedoms

**Document:**

* `pancakes_charter_of_rights.md`

The Charter defines foundational rights and freedoms for humane life computing.

Examples include:

* dignity,
* cognitive privacy,
* domestic sovereignty,
* local operation,
* export rights,
* symbolic expression,
* protection from extraction,
* community governance.

The Charter answers:

> What must always be protected?

---

# Layer 2: Human Flourishing Framework

**Document:**

* `human-flourishing-framework.md`

The Human Flourishing Framework defines the conditions and capacities that support flourishing across:

* individuals,
* households,
* communities,
* institutions,
* ecosystems,
* future generations.

It identifies domains such as:

* viability,
* safety,
* agency,
* competence,
* belonging,
* purpose,
* stewardship,
* participation,
* resilience.

The framework answers:

> What are we trying to cultivate?

---

# Layer 3: Common Good Model

**Document:**

* `pancakes-common-good-model.md`

The Common Good Model extends flourishing into collective life.

It evaluates how systems affect:

* agency,
* belonging,
* stewardship,
* dignity,
* reciprocity,
* resilience.

The model answers:

> How does this affect communities and collective flourishing?

---

# Layer 4: Stewardship Model

**Document:**

* `pancakes-stewardship-model.md`

The Stewardship Model defines responsibility throughout the ecosystem.

It addresses stewardship of:

* self,
* relationships,
* households,
* communities,
* institutions,
* ecosystems,
* information,
* AI systems.

The model answers:

> Who is responsible for caring for this?

---

# Layer 5: Standards Model

**Document:**

* `pancakes-standards-model.md`

The Standards Model determines which standards, regulations, frameworks, and governance requirements apply to a project.

Applicability is determined through a:

**Standards Applicability Profile (SAP)**

rather than through project names or marketing descriptions.

The model answers:

> Which standards and governance requirements apply?

---

# Layer 6: Safety Cases Framework

**Document:**

* `pancakes-safety-cases-framework.md`

Safety cases provide structured arguments explaining why a system should be considered acceptably safe and trustworthy.

Safety cases contain:

* claims,
* hazards,
* controls,
* evidence,
* residual risks,
* monitoring requirements.

The framework answers:

> Why should this system be considered acceptably safe?

# Governance Review Process

Major projects should generally follow the following workflow:

```text
Project Concept
        ↓
Standards Applicability Profile
        ↓
Standards Assessment
        ↓
Safety Case Assessment
        ↓
Ethics Assessment
        ↓
Privacy Review
        ↓
Stewardship Review
        ↓
Common Good Review
        ↓
Governance Decision
```

The goal is not to maximize paperwork.

The goal is to identify:

* harms,
* incentives,
* responsibilities,
* governance needs,
* and acceptable boundaries

before deployment.

# Emerging Issues

Governance reviews should not be treated as one-time exercises.

Projects may require reassessment when new information emerges.

Examples include:

* newly discovered hazards,
* newly recognized privacy risks,
* harmful incentive structures,
* regulatory changes,
* new AI capabilities,
* changes in social expectations,
* evidence of exploitation,
* emerging forms of flourishing.

The ecosystem assumes that understanding evolves over time.

Governance must evolve with it.

# Strategic Goal

The long-term objective of Pancakes governance is to help create systems that are:

* humane,
* trustworthy,
* privacy-preserving,
* stewardable,
* locally governable,
* economically non-exploitative,
* supportive of flourishing,
* and capable of adapting to future knowledge.

Governance exists to help computation remain accountable to people, households, communities, and future generations.

Technology should serve flourishing.

Not the reverse.
