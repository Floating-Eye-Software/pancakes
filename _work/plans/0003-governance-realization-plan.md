# 0003-Governance-Realization-Plan

## Status

Draft

## Purpose

This document defines the implementation plan for realizing the Pancakes Governance System.

The governance philosophy, principles, and major governance domains already exist. The remaining work is primarily operationalization:

* converting governance concepts into repeatable processes,
* creating reusable governance artifacts,
* integrating governance with the FLEY Quality Management System (QMS),
* establishing governance review workflows,
* creating reference assessments,
* and building institutional memory through precedents and registries.

This document serves as the implementation roadmap for making governance an operational capability rather than a collection of conceptual documents.

---

# Background

The Pancakes governance stack currently includes:

```text
FLEY Organizational Identity
        ↓
Pancakes Charter of Rights and Freedoms
        ↓
Human Flourishing Framework
        ↓
Common Good Model
        ↓
Stewardship Model
        ↓
Ethics Framework
        ↓
Standards Model
        ↓
Safety Cases Framework
        ↓
Privacy & Security
        ↓
Data Sovereignty
        ↓
Non-Exploitative Infrastructure
```

These documents define:

* values,
* rights,
* responsibilities,
* standards,
* safety principles,
* privacy principles,
* ethical principles,
* governance philosophy.

What remains is transforming those principles into operational governance processes.

---

# Strategic Goal

Create a governance system capable of answering:

```text
Should this project exist?

Under what conditions?

Which standards apply?

Which risks are acceptable?

Which ethical concerns exist?

How are people protected?

How are communities protected?

How are incentives governed?

How are rights preserved?

How is power constrained?

How does governance influence implementation?
```

while remaining compatible with the FLEY QMS and future regulated development processes.

---

# Governance Architecture Vision

The long-term governance workflow is:

```text
Project Concept
        ↓
Governance Intake
        ↓
Standards Applicability Profile (SAP)
        ↓
Standards Determination
        ↓
Safety Case Determination
        ↓
Ethics Determination
        ↓
Governance Assessment
        ↓
Governance Approval
        ↓
PQP / DCP Planning
        ↓
Execution
        ↓
Verification of Effectiveness
        ↓
Management Review
```

Governance precedes planning.

Planning precedes implementation.

Governance establishes constraints.

The QMS implements them.

# Success Criteria

The governance realization effort is considered successful when:

* New projects begin with a completed SAP.
* Governance reviews occur before implementation planning.
* Governance outputs feed directly into PQPs and DCPs.
* Safety Cases and Ethics Cases are routinely used.
* Governance decisions are traceable.
* Governance precedents are maintained.
* Reassessments occur when intended use changes.
* Governance findings feed Management Review.
* The ecosystem can consistently review new projects without inventing governance processes from scratch.

---

# Strategic Principle

The goal is not to create bureaucracy.

The goal is to create a practical governance capability that helps ensure Pancakes systems remain:

* humane,
* stewardable,
* privacy-preserving,
* non-exploitative,
* auditable,
* adaptable,
* and aligned with human flourishing.

Governance should make good decisions easier, harmful decisions harder, and future learning possible.
