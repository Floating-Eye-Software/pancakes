# Pancakes Ethics Governance Roadmap

## Purpose

This document outlines the remaining ethics and humane-governance work required to complete the Pancakes governance stack.

The ecosystem already contains substantial work related to:

* rights,
* flourishing,
* stewardship,
* common good,
* privacy,
* data sovereignty,
* standards,
* safety,
* risk management.

The newly created Ethics Framework establishes a mechanism for ethical review and ethical decision-making.

The purpose of this roadmap is to identify the remaining governance artifacts required to operationalize that framework.

---

# Current Governance Stack

Current state:

```text
FLEY Organizational Identity
        ↓

        ↓
Human Flourishing Framework
        ↓

        ↓
Stewardship Model
        ↓
Ethics Framework
        ↓
Standards Model
        ↓

```

Missing pieces exist primarily below the Ethics Framework.

---

# Strategic Goal

Create a governance system capable of answering:

```text
Should this project exist?

Under what conditions?

Who benefits?

Who bears risk?

How are participants protected?

How are communities protected?

How is dignity preserved?

How are incentives governed?

How is power constrained?
```

across all current and future Pancakes projects.

---

# Phase 1 — Core Ethics Infrastructure

Priority: High

These documents are required before most future ecosystem projects can be reviewed consistently.

---

## 1. Pancakes Ethics Cases

### Purpose

Provide the ethics equivalent of Safety Cases.

Ethics Cases justify:

* ethical acceptability,
* participant protections,
* governance controls,
* incentive structures,
* residual ethical risk.

### Relationship

```text
Ethics Framework
        ↓
Ethics Cases
```

### Deliverables

* Ethics Case template
* Ethics Case lifecycle
* Review workflow
* Approval workflow
* Monitoring requirements

### Example Cases

* Red Witch
* Addiction Observatory
* Service Exchange
* AI Mentor
* Community Data Trust

---

## 2. Pancakes Research Ethics Standard

* data products,

* compensation.

### Topics

# Open Questions

Several unresolved questions appear repeatedly throughout ecosystem design.

### Research

Can communities ethically create data products?

### Compensation

How should participants share in value created from their contributions?

### AI

How much authority should AI systems possess?

### Symbolism

What ethical responsibilities arise when software uses mythic, symbolic, or narrative frameworks?

### Stewardship

How should stewardship obligations be reviewed and enforced?

### Community Governance

What ethical authority belongs to nodes, guilds, cooperatives, and institutions?

# Long-Term Goal

The objective is not to create an academic ethics bureaucracy.

The objective is to create governance systems that help the ecosystem remain aligned with:

* dignity,
* autonomy,
* stewardship,
* reciprocity,
* participation,
* common good,
* human flourishing,

as the ecosystem grows in complexity.

Technology changes.

Projects change.

Governance structures change.

The ethical commitments of the ecosystem should remain understandable, reviewable, and durable across those changes.
