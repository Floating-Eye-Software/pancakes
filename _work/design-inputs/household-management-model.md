What's interesting is that this maps surprisingly well onto the Pancakes domains you've been converging on.

For example, the RPG spec eventually reduced real life into domains like:

* Movement
* Rest
* Nourishment
* Household Stewardship
* Study and Work
* Reflection
* Care and Connection



And the broader Pancakes philosophy keeps returning to the idea that most of human life consists of a relatively small number of recurring stewardship functions: food, care, maintenance, learning, movement, rest, administration, and community participation.

If I were building a household operating system from scratch, I'd probably compress the entire thing even further into:

1. Food
2. Shelter
3. Health
4. Money
5. Logistics
6. Relationships
7. Learning
8. Community
9. Governance
10. Future Planning

Everything else is a sub-process of one of those.

My guess is the conversation you're remembering was somewhere around that level: "the household is not 500 chores; it's about a dozen systems, each producing an endless stream of chores." That's a very different mental model.

     - Before and after preparing food
     - Before eating or touching food
     - After using the restroom
     - After coughing, sneezing, or blowing your nose
     - After touching pets or handling waste
   - Use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol if soap and water are not readily available

3. Bathing and Showering Routine:
   - Bathe or shower regularly to maintain overall cleanliness
   - Use a mild soap or body wash to cleanse the body
   - Pay attention to cleaning areas prone to sweat and odor (e.g., underarms, feet)
   - Rinse thoroughly and dry off with a clean towel to prevent moisture-related issues

4. Hair Care Routine:
   - Shampoo and condition hair regularly, as per individual hair type and needs
   - Rinse hair thoroughly to remove all product residues
   - Brush or comb hair regularly to keep it tangle-free and well-groomed
   - Trim hair regularly to maintain healthy ends and prevent split ends
