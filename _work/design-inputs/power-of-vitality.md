# Power of Vitality

## A Design Archaeology for Pancakes

**Status:** Design Input

**Audience:** Pancakes architecture, Lifecraft, Household Management, Service Exchange, Enterprise

**Purpose:** Preserve valuable institutional and interaction design ideas from Power of Vitality while critically evaluating them through the philosophy of Pancakes.

---

# Executive Summary

Power of Vitality represents one of the most sophisticated commercial wellbeing
platforms developed during the 2010s and early 2020s.

Originally created within the Discovery/Vitality ecosystem and deployed through
large employers (including IBM), it combined:

- behavioral science
- actuarial modeling
- coaching
- personalized guidance
- recurring games
- long-term identity
- rewards
- healthcare integration

into a coherent institutional platform.

Unlike many wellness applications, Power of Vitality was not merely a fitness
tracker or rewards program.

It attempted to become a long-lived institution that accompanied members
through years of ordinary life.

Although the platform ultimately existed within an insurance and employer
wellness ecosystem whose incentives differ substantially from Pancakes, many of
its architectural discoveries remain valuable.

This document treats Power of Vitality as an archaeological artifact rather
than a product to imitate.

---

# Sources and Reconstruction

The original member experience is no longer publicly available.

This analysis is reconstructed from:

- surviving product brochures
- marketing material
- Care Connector documentation
- historical screenshots
- long-term user recollections
- public descriptions of the platform

Some of the most interesting components—including recurring physician lectures,
seasonal programming, and detailed game mechanics—appear to have existed only
inside the member portal.

Consequently parts of this document represent informed reconstruction rather
than verified historical fact.

Where appropriate, recollections are explicitly identified as tentative.

---

# Historical Context

Power of Vitality emerged from Discovery's shared-value insurance model.

The underlying economic assumption was simple.

Healthier members reduce long-term healthcare costs.

Some portion of those savings can therefore be shared with members through
rewards that encourage healthier behavior.

This produced a platform that attempted to align incentives among:

- members
- employers
- insurers
- healthcare providers

Its economic model is not directly applicable to Pancakes.

Its interaction design frequently is.

---

# A Behavioral Institution

The most important observation is that Power of Vitality should not primarily
be understood as software.

It functioned as an institution.

It combined:

- recurring educational programming
- coaching
- physician communication
- employer initiatives
- seasonal campaigns
- long-term identity
- recurring rituals
- rewards

Software supported the institution.

The institution sustained engagement with the software.

---

# Architectural Model

The modern platform presents itself using three behavioral phases.

```text
Activate

↓

Engage

↓

Reinforce
```

This is a remarkably elegant architecture.

Rather than organizing around software modules, it organizes around the human
process of behavior change.

---

# Activate

Activation establishes personal relevance.

Recovered components include:

- Vitality Health Review
- Health Profile
- Vitality Age
- personalized communications
- initial assessments

The objective is not merely collecting data.

It is helping members discover:

> What should I work on first?

The system attempts to reduce uncertainty while creating immediate momentum.

---

# Engage

Engagement moves from assessment toward participation.

Recovered capabilities include:

- personalized activities
- focused health topics
- challenges
- coaching
- Care Connector
- condition management

Notice the architectural shift.

The software increasingly behaves less like a tracker and more like a
coordinator.

---

# Reinforce

The reinforcement layer contains multiple motivational systems operating
simultaneously.

Recovered systems include:

- Vitality Points
- Vitality Status
- Vitality Squares
- Active Rewards
- Vitality Wheel
- Instant Rewards
- Rewards Mall
- Apple Watch earning program

This is not one incentive system.

It is several overlapping systems operating on different time scales.

---

# Multiple Time Horizons

One of Power of Vitality's strongest architectural ideas is recognizing that
motivation operates on different clocks.

Immediate:

- instant rewards

Weekly:

- physical activity goals
- Active Rewards
- Wheel spins

Monthly:

- Vitality Squares
- physician lectures
- seasonal campaigns

Long-term:

- Vitality Status
- accumulated identity
- years of participation

Most wellness applications choose one.

Power of Vitality deliberately combined all of them.

# Institutional Continuity

Perhaps the platform's most overlooked achievement was continuity.

Users interacted repeatedly with:

- the same physician
- the same platform
- recurring games
- recurring campaigns
- long-term status progression

Many modern applications optimize novelty.

Power of Vitality optimized familiarity.

That distinction matters.

---

# The Physician

One recovered recollection deserves special attention.

A physician hosted recurring educational presentations.

User recollection suggests:

- monthly cadence
- approximately 30–45 minutes
- current public health topics
- ongoing continuity across many years

One memorable anecdote involved the physician noting that analytics showed one
anonymous participant had watched every lecture ever produced and greeting that
unknown viewer.

Remarkably, this anecdote remains memorable years later despite the individual
lecture topics being forgotten.

This suggests the lectures' value may have come less from individual content
than from institutional continuity.

The physician became part of the platform's identity.

---

# Care Connector

Care Connector represents another notable institutional component.

Rather than replacing healthcare providers, it strengthened relationships among:

- members
- coaches
- care managers
- providers

The software became connective infrastructure rather than merely another app.

This distinction aligns closely with Pancakes' philosophy.

Software should coordinate human relationships rather than replace them.

---

# Vitality Squares

Only fragments of Vitality Squares survive.

Public documentation describes it only as a monthly game connected with rewards.

However, long-term user recollections suggest the following interaction model.

---

## Reconstructed Interaction (Tentative)

The following reconstruction is based upon participant memory rather than
surviving documentation.

Approximately once each month:

- members received access to a concealed 5×5 grid
- players initially received three selections
- revealing tiles uncovered food items
- foods included healthy and unhealthy examples
- healthier collections produced better rewards
- participation throughout the month could earn an additional selection

Although individual details remain uncertain, the overall interaction appears
clear.

Ordinary healthy behavior improved one's position in a recurring monthly game.

---

# Why Squares Was Interesting

Squares did not simply award points.

It transformed ongoing participation into anticipation.

```text
daily activity

↓

monthly engagement

↓

extra reveal

↓

monthly ritual

↓

points

↓

status
```

The reward was delayed.

Members looked forward to the monthly event.

This differs psychologically from immediate reinforcement.

---

# Symbolic Design

If recollections are accurate, the hidden objects were foods rather than
generic tokens.

This matters.

The game communicated nutritional ideas symbolically rather than through
lectures.

Healthy foods became associated with success.

The educational layer and the game layer reinforced one another.

---

# The Rewards Were the Hook

One of the strongest recovered observations concerns rewards.

Employees generally did not become excited about an employer wellness portal.

They became excited because Vitality Points could be redeemed for Amazon gift
cards.

This mattered enormously.

Unlike many corporate incentive systems, the rewards possessed genuine
flexibility.

Members could buy things they actually wanted.

The rewards were economically meaningful.

For many participants, this was the reason they first engaged with the system.

---

# Why Amazon Mattered

Traditional employer wellness portals often rewarded participants with
curated merchandise.

Power of Vitality instead offered something much closer to fungible value.

The participant retained agency.

The software rewarded healthy behavior without prescribing how the reward
should ultimately be used.

This subtle preservation of autonomy likely contributed significantly to the
platform's appeal.

---

# Extrinsic and Intrinsic Motivation

Power of Vitality demonstrates something more interesting than "gamification."

The rewards attracted participation.

The institution sustained it.

Over time participants remembered:

- Vitality Squares
- physician lectures
- recurring rituals
- accumulated status

far more vividly than individual rewards.

This suggests an important progression.

```text
economic reward

↓

continued participation

↓

ritual

↓

identity

↓

institutional belonging
```

Extrinsic motivation opened the door.

Institutional continuity kept people engaged.

# What Pancakes Can Learn

Power of Vitality answered one question.

> How can software encourage healthier behavior?

Pancakes asks a broader one.

> How can software help cultivate flourishing lives?

The difference is substantial.

Power of Vitality focused primarily upon:

- health
- exercise
- nutrition
- screenings
- healthcare utilization

Pancakes extends this toward:

- households
- caregiving
- stewardship
- learning
- creativity
- community
- service
- human flourishing

Nevertheless many architectural ideas remain valuable.

## Institutions Matter

People remember recurring humans.

Not notifications.

---

## Multiple Time Horizons

Behavior change operates simultaneously on daily, weekly, monthly, seasonal,
and lifetime scales.

---

## Play Matters

Squares demonstrates that delight belongs inside serious systems.

---

## Continuity Matters

---

## Human Coordination Matters

# What Pancakes Should Reject

Power of Vitality also reflects its origins.

Pancakes should reject:

- employer surveillance
- actuarial optimization
- insurance-centered incentives
- fear-of-missing-out design
- treating participants primarily as healthcare cost centers

The objective is not behavioral optimization.

It is stewardship and flourishing.

---

# From Rewards to Recognition

The deepest difference concerns economics.

Power of Vitality begins with externally funded incentives.

```text
insurance savings

↓

gift cards
```

Pancakes begins with invisible contribution.

```text
household work

↓

recognition

↓

voluntary settlement

↓

agency
```

Rather than paying people to behave well, Pancakes seeks to make valuable work
that already exists economically legible.

This distinction is fundamental.

---

# Relationship to Service Exchange

Power of Vitality rewarded activities using centrally designed incentive
structures.

Pancakes Service Exchange seeks something different.

Invisible labor becomes visible.

Communities voluntarily settle contributions.

Repeated settlements gradually reveal local understandings of value.

Rather than assigning prices, Service Exchange discovers them.

The result is not a wellness reward program.

It is an institution for recognizing work that has historically remained
economically invisible.

---

# Escape Funds

One implication deserves particular attention.

People who perform years of unpaid care work frequently accumulate little
independent economic security.

If voluntary settlements become possible, contributors may gradually build
personal assets under their own control.

These assets remain:

- portable
- private
- independently owned

This creates what might be called an escape fund.

Its purpose is not dissolving households.

Its purpose is preserving agency.

Institutions should increase the number of futures available to participants.

---

# Design Lessons

Power of Vitality succeeded because it combined:

- education
- play
- coaching
- identity
- recurring rituals
- human continuity
- layered motivation
- rewards

into a coherent institution.

The mechanics supported the institution.

The institution sustained the mechanics.

Pancakes should preserve the institutional architecture while replacing the
economic and philosophical assumptions that originally motivated it.

---

# Conclusion

Power of Vitality should not be remembered primarily as a gamified wellness
application.

It should be remembered as an ambitious attempt to build a long-lived
institution that helped people make healthier choices over many years.

Pancakes inherits a different mission.

Rather than optimizing health behaviors for employers and insurers, it seeks
to build institutions that recognize invisible labor, strengthen households,
support communities, preserve agency, and cultivate human flourishing.

The enduring lesson is therefore not about points, badges, or gift cards.

It is that software can become a trusted institution when it combines
continuity, recurring rituals, human relationships, meaningful recognition,
and playful interaction into a coherent architecture that accompanies people
through ordinary life.
