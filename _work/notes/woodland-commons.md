# Woodland Commons

## Conceptual Design Brief

### Project Vision

Woodland Commons is envisioned as a small residential community organized around a large underground shared commons concealed beneath a naturalistic woodland landscape.

The project seeks to combine three distinct experiences:

1. A woodland park with mature trees, trails, and a flowing stream.
2. A small village composed of detached residences and community amenities.
3. A substantial underground commons that serves as the social, recreational, and operational heart of the community.

The primary objective is to preserve the beauty and tranquility of the landscape while concentrating much of the community's shared infrastructure beneath the surface.

Visitors should first experience trees, water, trails, and homes. Only later should they discover the larger world beneath the landscape.

---

## Design Principles

### Landscape First

The landscape shall be the dominant visual feature of the property.

Buildings, infrastructure, parking, and service functions should be designed to support the landscape rather than compete with it.

The property should feel more like a woodland preserve with residences than a conventional residential development.

### Discovery Rather Than Secrecy

The underground commons is not intended to be hidden in a secretive sense.

Entrances should be visible, welcoming, and easily understood.

The sense of discovery should come from the contrast between the modest scale of the entrances and the unexpectedly large shared spaces beneath the surface.

### Human Scale

The surface environment should remain intimate and comfortable.

Small buildings, pedestrian pathways, woodland clearings, footbridges, gardens, and gathering spaces should define the character of the community.

### Long-Term Stewardship

The project should prioritize durability, maintainability, environmental responsibility, and long-term resilience.

All systems should be designed with multi-generational ownership and use in mind.

---

## Site Organization

The property should be organized into four interconnected zones:

### Woodland Core

A central grove of mature trees shall form the symbolic heart of the community.

The grove should contain several entrances to the underground commons and serve as a primary gathering space.

### Residential Village

Small detached residences should be distributed around the woodland core.

The village should feel informal and organic rather than rigidly planned.

Residences should be oriented toward landscape features, walking paths, and the stream corridor.

### Stream Corridor

A naturalized stream shall serve as a primary organizing element of the site.

The stream should provide ecological, recreational, and aesthetic value while helping define the identity of the community.

### Service District

Parking, loading, maintenance, utility, and transportation infrastructure should be located near the edge of the property and screened from primary residential and recreational areas.

---

## Surface Environment

Desired surface features include:

* Detached residences or tiny houses.
* Woodland trails.
* Native plantings.
* Community gardens.
* Small gathering spaces.
* Benches and overlooks.
* Footbridges.
* Natural play and recreation areas.
* Streamside seating areas.
* Open lawns and meadows where appropriate.

The surface environment should appear natural, mature, and well integrated with the surrounding landscape.

---

## Stream and Water Features

The stream shall be considered a core project feature rather than a decorative amenity.

Design objectives include:

* Naturalistic appearance.
* Year-round flow where feasible.
* Native riparian vegetation.
* Habitat creation.
* Integration with stormwater management.
* Walking paths along portions of the stream.
* Small bridges and crossings.
* Opportunities for quiet contemplation and recreation.

The sound of moving water should contribute significantly to the atmosphere of the property.

The stream should appear to have shaped the site naturally over time.

---

## Trail System

A network of interconnected walking paths shall connect all portions of the property.

A dedicated loop trail with a precisely surveyed length of 400 meters shall be incorporated into the design.

The 400-meter loop should:

* Support walking and jogging.
* Pass through the most attractive portions of the site.
* Include portions adjacent to the stream corridor and woodland grove.
* Be usable year-round.
* Include subtle distance markers.

The loop should feel like a natural woodland trail rather than an athletic facility.

---

## Underground Commons

The underground commons shall serve as the social and operational center of the community.

Potential program elements include:

### Community Spaces

* Great hall.
* Community kitchen.
* Dining facilities.
* Lounge areas.
* Library and reading rooms.
* Meeting rooms.
* Multi-purpose gathering spaces.

### Recreation

* Fitness room.
* Hobby rooms.
* Media spaces.
* Indoor recreation areas.

### Guest Facilities

* Guest suites.
* Visitor accommodations.
* Shared washrooms and support facilities.

### Workshop Areas

* Maker space.
* Woodworking and fabrication areas.
* Tool storage.
* Maintenance facilities.

### Storage and Operations

* General storage.
* Utility rooms.
* Mechanical systems.
* Electrical systems.
* Building management facilities.

The underground environment should avoid the character of a bunker.

Natural light, generous ceiling heights, warm materials, and visual connections to landscape should be incorporated wherever possible.

---

## Entrances

Entrances to the underground commons should take the form of modest freestanding structures located within the woodland core.

Preferred characteristics include:

* Approximately pavilion or utility-building scale.
* Durable construction.
* Distinct architectural identity.
* Clear visibility.
* Simple wayfinding.
* Integration with landscape design.

Each entrance should contain stairs and, where appropriate, elevator access.

The entrances should suggest the existence of a larger underground environment without revealing its full scale.

---

## Specialized Gaming and Media Room

A dedicated gaming and media room shall be included within the underground commons.

Requirements include:

* Acoustic isolation.
* Independent ventilation and cooling systems.
* Robust network infrastructure.
* Flexible furniture arrangements.
* Capacity for multiplayer gaming, media events, and community gatherings.
* Long-duration occupant comfort.

While recreational in nature, this room is considered a permanent and important program element.

---

## Transportation and Utility Infrastructure

The project shall include:

### Parking

* Resident parking.
* Visitor parking.
* Bicycle parking.

### Electric Vehicle Charging

* Multiple Level 2 charging stations.
* Expansion capability for future demand.

### RV Accommodation

* Dedicated RV parking pad.
* Electrical hookups.
* Water service.
* Sewer connection.

### Loading and Deliveries

A dedicated loading dock shall be located within the service district.

The loading area should include:

* Truck access.
* Freight handling space.
* Direct underground connection.
* Freight elevator access.
* Storage receiving facilities.

All major deliveries should be capable of reaching underground facilities without passing through residential or public gathering areas.

---

## Environmental Systems

Particular attention should be given to:

* Groundwater management.
* Waterproofing.
* Drainage systems.
* Ventilation.
* Indoor air quality.
* Thermal performance.
* Energy efficiency.
* Renewable energy integration.
* Resilience during utility disruptions.

The project should explore opportunities for geothermal systems, battery storage, solar generation, and high-performance building envelopes.

---

## Life Safety

The design shall prioritize:

* Multiple independent exits.
* Fire protection systems.
* Smoke management.
* Emergency lighting.
* Accessibility.
* Emergency vehicle access.
* Long-term operational resilience.

Life safety systems should be integrated in a manner that does not compromise the character of the community.

---

## Desired Character

The completed project should feel like a place that has grown naturally around a stream in a woodland setting.

Residents should experience:

* The sound of water.
* The shade of trees.
* Walkable paths.
* Small-scale homes.
* Shared community life.
* Spaces for hobbies and recreation.
* Strong connections to nature.

Visitors should arrive expecting a small woodland village and gradually discover that beneath the grove lies a much larger shared commons supporting the life of the community.

The overall atmosphere should be one of stewardship, curiosity, craftsmanship, friendship, and quiet wonder.
