# Woodland Commons Minecraft Build Plan

## Draft 1: Regional Layout and Terrain Plan

### Purpose

This first draft focuses entirely on the overall shape of Woodland Commons.

Do not build detailed structures yet.

The goal is to establish:

* terrain
* water
* forests
* pathways
* building sites
* underground footprint

before any architectural detailing.

---

# World Size

Recommended build area:

```text
512 x 512 blocks
```

This is large enough to feel like a real settlement while remaining manageable.

Approximate regions:

```text
Woodland Core
150 x 150

Stepwell District
100 x 100

Residential Village
250 x 250

Stream Corridor
Entire map

Commons Footprint
120 x 120 underground

Service District
100 x 100
```

---

# Terrain Concept

The terrain should resemble a wooded river valley.

Imagine:

```text
higher ground
     ↓
forest ridge
     ↓
stream valley
     ↓
forest ridge
```

Avoid flat terrain.

Create gentle elevation changes.

The stream should feel ancient and natural.

---

# Region 1: Woodland Core

The symbolic center of the community.

Located near the middle of the map.

Features:

* large mature forest
* winding trails
* clearings
* benches
* entrance pavilions
* access to stepwell

Shape:

```text
rough circle
150 blocks diameter
```

This area should feel peaceful and slightly mysterious.

Players should naturally wander toward it.

---

# Region 2: Stepwell

The civic heart.

Located directly within the Woodland Core.

This is the first thing visitors remember.

Diameter:

```text
60-80 blocks
```

Shape:

```text
large circular excavation
```

Layers:

```text
forest
↓
terraces
↓
gardens
↓
water basin
↓
glass commons entrances
```

The stream should pass through or beside the stepwell.

The stepwell should be visible from many points in the woodland.

---

# Region 3: Stream Corridor

The stream is one of the most important features.

The stream should:

* enter from map edge
* meander naturally
* pass the stepwell
* continue toward opposite edge

Features:

* bridges
* riparian vegetation
* small waterfalls
* overlooks
* footpaths

The stream should organize the entire settlement.

---

# Region 4: Residential Village

Located around the Woodland Core.

Design goals:

* informal layout
* houses facing trails
* houses facing water
* no rigid grid

Build:

* cabins
* cottages
* tiny homes
* woodland houses

Spacing:

```text
20-40 blocks between homes
```

Residents should feel connected but not crowded.

---

# Region 5: Service District

Located at edge of map.

Features:

* parking
* deliveries
* maintenance
* utilities
* freight elevator access

This area should be functional rather than beautiful.

Hide it with trees.

---

# Region 6: 400m Loop Trail

One complete loop.

Route should pass:

* woodland
* stream
* village
* stepwell

Use:

* gravel
* coarse dirt
* path blocks

The loop should feel like a woodland trail.

Not a racetrack.

---

# Region 7: Underground Commons Footprint

Do not excavate details yet.

Just reserve space.

Suggested footprint:

```text
120 x 120
```

Directly beneath:

* Woodland Core
* Stepwell

This becomes the hidden heart of the community.

---

# Build Order

1. Terrain
2. Stream
3. Forest
4. Stepwell
5. Trails
6. Village lots
7. Commons footprint
8. Buildings
9. Details

Never reverse this order.

The landscape is the primary structure.
