# Woodland Commons Minecraft Construction Guide

## Phase 1: Create the Valley

### Step 1: Find a Suitable Seed

Look for:

* forest biome
* river biome
* hills
* spruce or mixed woodland

Do not start in:

* plains
* desert
* jungle
* mountain peaks

You want a gentle wooded valley.

---

## Step 2: Claim a 512 x 512 Area

Turn on chunk borders if needed.

Mark:

```text
North edge
South edge
East edge
West edge
```

with temporary pillars.

The entire project fits inside this square.

---

## Step 3: Build the Stream First

This is the most important step.

Start at one edge of the map.

Dig a stream that:

* curves naturally
* never forms straight lines
* widens occasionally
* narrows occasionally

Width:

```text
4-8 blocks
```

Depth:

```text
2-4 blocks
```

Do not worry about houses.

Do not worry about roads.

Everything follows the stream.

---

## Step 4: Create the Woodland Core

Find the center of the map.

Mark a circle:

```text
150 block diameter
```

This becomes the grove.

Plant:

* oak
* spruce
* birch

Leave occasional clearings.

The grove should feel old.

Dense but walkable.

---

## Step 5: Lay Out the 400m Loop

Build a trail that:

* crosses the stream
* enters woodland
* skirts village areas
* passes the future stepwell

Use:

* path blocks
* coarse dirt
* gravel

Make it winding.

Not circular.

Measure roughly 400 blocks total length.

---

# Phase 2: Build the Stepwell

This is the centerpiece.

Everything else can wait.

---

## Step 6: Mark the Circle

Diameter:

```text
70 blocks
```

Place temporary blocks around perimeter.

Stand in the center.

Verify views.

Adjust if necessary.

---

## Step 7: Excavate

Dig downward.

Depth:

```text
24 blocks
```

Leave terraces every:

```text
4 blocks vertical
```

Example:

Level 0

70 blocks wide

↓

Level -4

60 blocks wide

↓

Level -8

50 blocks wide

↓

Level -12

40 blocks wide

↓

Level -16

30 blocks wide

↓

Level -20

20 blocks wide

↓

Level -24

central basin


You are creating a giant inverted pyramid.

---

## Step 8: Shape Terraces

Do not make perfect geometry.

Break edges.

Add:

- planters
- benches
- retaining walls
- gardens

Imagine hundreds of people sitting here.

---

## Step 9: Route the Stream

The stream should enter the stepwell.

Options:

### Option A

Cross the upper terraces.

### Option B

Waterfall into lower basin.

### Option C

Pass beside the stepwell wall.

Personally I would choose:

```text
small waterfall
→ lower reflecting pool
````

---

# Phase 3: Commons Excavation

Now build underground.

---

## Step 10: Find the Commons Center

Stand at bottom of stepwell.

This becomes the center of the underground complex.

---

## Step 11: Excavate Main Hall

Size:

```text
120 x 120
```

Height:

```text
12 blocks
```

Do not detail it.

Just excavate.

The scale should surprise you.

---

## Step 12: Create Light Wells

This is critical.

At least four.

Diameter:

```text
12-16 blocks
```

Extend from commons ceiling to surface.

Fill with:

* trees
* glass
* gardens

Without these, the commons becomes a bunker.

---

# Phase 4: Great Hall

Build first interior room.

---

## Step 13: Great Hall Layout

Size:

```text
40 x 40
```

Ceiling:

```text
12 blocks high
```

Features:

* fireplace
* tables
* lounge
* balcony

Materials:

* spruce
* dark oak
* stone
* copper

Think:

```text
university commons
```

not:

```text
medieval castle
```

---

## Step 14: Connect to Stepwell

Build grand staircases.

Several routes.

Never a single entrance.

People should wander naturally.

---

# Phase 5: Build Specialized Areas

Only now.

---

## Library

25 x 30

Bookshelves.

Reading corners.

Windows into light wells.

---

## Kitchen

Connected directly to Great Hall.

Large communal tables nearby.

---

## Workshop

Near service district.

High ceilings.

Storage walls.

Crafting stations.

---

## Guest Suites

Quiet zone.

Far from gaming room.

---

# Phase 6: Gaming Bunker

The sacred chamber.

---

## Placement

Deepest commons location.

Farthest from library.

Farthest from guest rooms.

---

## Excavation

30 x 30

Height:

8-10 blocks

---

## Materials

Blackstone.

Deepslate.

Copper.

Redstone lighting.

Server-room aesthetic.

---

## Features

LAN tables.

Projection wall.

Arcade area.

Redstone showcase.

Map wall.

Trophy room.

---

## Atmosphere

The rest of Woodland Commons should feel:

```text
nature
craftsmanship
community
```

The gaming bunker should feel:

```text
technology
experimentation
late-night energy
```

A completely different mood.

---

# Phase 7: Surface Village

Only after all civic spaces exist.

This is important.

The community comes first.

Housing comes second.

---

## Build 12-20 Homes

Mix:

* cabins
* cottages
* modern woodland houses

Place them organically.

Not subdivisions.

---

## Face Houses Toward

* trails
* stream
* grove

Never toward roads.

---

# Phase 8: Details

The final pass.

---

Add:

* lanterns
* signs
* bridges
* flower beds
* benches
* gazebos
* picnic tables
* docks
* overlooks

Do not over-light.

Allow darkness in some areas.

Allow mystery.

---

# Final Test

Start at the edge of the map.

Walk into Woodland Commons.

You should experience:

Forest

↓

Stream

↓

Trail

↓

Stepwell

↓

Glass Pavilion

↓

Commons

↓

Great Hall

↓

Gaming Bunker

If that journey feels magical, the build is successful.

If people immediately notice the buildings instead of the landscape, return to Phase 1 and add more trees.
