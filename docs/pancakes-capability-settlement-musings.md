# Pancakes Capability Settlement Musings

**Status:** Exploratory design notes

This document explores possible architectures for recognizing and settling
valuable contributions within Pancakes Service Exchange.

Unlike the Pancakes economic theory documents, this document intentionally
remains speculative.

The purpose is not to define the architecture.

The purpose is to explore the design space.

---

# The Initial Assumption

The simplest mental model looks like this.

```text
Node A

↓

records activity

↓

Node B

↓

pays for activity
```

For example:

```text
Cook dinner

↓

10 credits
```

This resembles a conventional labor market.

It is easy to understand.

It is also probably the least interesting possibility.

It reduces Service Exchange to gig work.

---

# Activities versus Capabilities

Perhaps the wrong thing is being exchanged.

Activities are individual events.

Capabilities are demonstrated patterns.

Preparing one meal is an activity.

Being capable of sustaining household nutrition for six months is a capability.

This distinction feels important.

---

# Activities Produce Evidence

Instead of becoming economic transactions directly, activities accumulate into
evidence.

```text
Cook meal

↓

Recorded activity

↓

Household Nutrition evidence

↓

Capability
```

The capability becomes the economically interesting object.

---

# Capabilities are Contextual

Capabilities need not be professional credentials.

Examples include:

- sustained household nutrition
- household administration
- caregiving
- elder care
- transportation coordination
- conflict mediation
- community gardening
- neighborhood stewardship
- emergency preparedness
- hospitality

Most of these have no conventional labor market despite producing substantial
social value.

---

# Recognition Instead of Purchase

Perhaps communities do not purchase activities.

Instead they recognize demonstrated capability.

For example:

```text
Community Kitchen

Recognizes:

Household Nutrition

Requirements:

100 qualifying meals
over at least 60 days

Recognition:

250 community credits
```

The organization is not buying meals.

The meals already happened.

The organization is recognizing demonstrated stewardship.

---

# The Household is not the Customer

This observation may be one of the most important.

Traditional labor markets assume:

```text
buyer

↓

seller
```

Household labor frequently lacks an identifiable buyer.

The beneficiary may be:

- children
- spouses
- aging parents
- neighbors
- the community
- future society

The value exists.

The purchaser does not.

This suggests that conventional labor market assumptions may be the wrong
foundation.

---

# The Community Benefits

Households generate many public goods.

Healthy children.

Healthy adults.

Stable neighborhoods.

Volunteer capacity.

Reduced healthcare utilization.

Community resilience.

Future caregivers.

Future workers.

These benefits extend beyond the household itself.

Perhaps communities therefore have legitimate reasons to recognize household
stewardship.

---

# Recognition Programs

Instead of publishing prices, organizations could publish recognition
programs.

Examples:

```text
Women's Cooperative

Recognizes

Sustained Caregiving

Benefits

Emergency grants
Transportation
Education support
```

```text
Food Bank

Recognizes

Household Nutrition

Benefits

Leadership opportunities
Volunteer stipends
```

```text
Neighborhood Association

Recognizes

Community Stewardship

Benefits

Tool library access
Garden funding
Microgrants
```

No one purchases cooking.

Cooking becomes evidence.

---

# Capability Exchange

Perhaps Service Exchange is fundamentally misnamed.

Perhaps services are not exchanged.

Capabilities are.

Organizations publish:

> We recognize these capabilities.

Households publish:

> We possess these capabilities.

Settlement follows.

---

# Capabilities are Private

Importantly, capabilities should not require exporting activity histories.

Instead:

Organization requests proof.

Node proves capability.

Activity history remains local.

For example:

```text
Requested capability

Household Nutrition Level 2

↓

Proof generated locally

↓

Capability verified

↓

Settlement
```

This aligns naturally with Pancakes' commitment to data sovereignty.

---

# Many Institutions

One capability may be valuable to many organizations simultaneously.

```text
Household Nutrition

↓

Women's Cooperative

↓

Food Bank

↓

Community Kitchen

↓

Mutual Aid Network

↓

Faith Community
```

Each organization establishes its own recognition policy.

No central authority determines value.

---

# Discovery Rather than Assignment

This architecture resembles Hayek's value discovery.

Organizations independently publish what they value.

Communities independently decide what to recognize.

Households independently choose what evidence to disclose.

Settlement emerges from interaction rather than central planning.

---

# Agency

One consequence deserves particular attention.

Historically, invisible labor often produces no independent assets.

Capability recognition changes this.

```text
Invisible labor

↓

Evidence

↓

Capability

↓

Recognition

↓

Settlement

↓

Personal assets
```

This creates agency.

Participants gradually accumulate resources under their own control.

---

# Escape Funds

One compelling application involves domestic abuse.

Years of invisible household work often produce little independent economic
security.

Recognition programs could gradually accumulate privately controlled
resources.

The purpose is not to encourage household dissolution.

The purpose is preserving meaningful exit options.

Institutions should increase the range of futures available to participants.

---

# Beyond Money

Settlement need not always involve money.

Possible forms include:

- grants
- community credits
- emergency assistance
- educational opportunities
- housing priority
- transportation support
- childcare
- respite care
- tool access
- cooperative dividends
- mutual aid
- portable reputation

Money is only one possible settlement mechanism.

---

# An Ecosystem Rather than a Market

Traditional markets resemble:

```text
buyer

↓

seller

↓

price
```

Capability settlement may instead resemble:

```text
contributors

↓

capabilities

↓

communities

↓

recognition

↓

settlement
```

This feels less like a labor market and more like an ecosystem of institutions.

---

# Open Questions

Many questions remain unanswered.

Should capabilities be standardized?

Who defines capabilities?

Can communities define their own?

How are capabilities updated?

Can capability requirements evolve?

Can organizations revoke recognition?

Should settlements recur automatically?

Can organizations sponsor capabilities?

Should capability proofs be anonymous?

Can one activity contribute to multiple capabilities?

Can capabilities expire?

Should communities publish capability "bounties" rather than prices?
