# pancakes-character-canon.md

## Purpose

This document defines the visual identity of recurring Pancakes personas.

It exists to ensure continuity across:

* concept art
* onboarding illustrations
* story scenes
* website graphics
* RPG content
* marketing materials
* educational documents

The goal is recognizability rather than realism.

Characters should remain identifiable even when:

* clothing changes
* environments change
* art styles vary
* life circumstances change

---

# Design Principles

## Silhouette Before Face

Characters should be identifiable primarily through:

1. silhouette
2. posture
3. color palette
4. clothing language
5. props

Faces are secondary.

Current image models frequently drift facial details.

Silhouettes remain more stable.

---

## Archetype Before Demographic

Characters represent recurring life perspectives.

Visual design should support their role.

Avoid reducing characters to demographic signifiers.

---

## Consistency Before Variety

Every character should have:

* signature colors
* signature accessories
* signature posture
* signature motifs

These should remain stable across generations.

---

# Art Direction

## Style

Preferred style:

```text
storybook realism
human warmth
illustrated
optimistic
grounded
detailed
non-corporate
```

Avoid:

```text
stock photography
advertising aesthetics
hypersexualization
influencer aesthetics
corporate illustration styles
```

---

## Tone

Characters should feel:

* competent
* approachable
* imperfect
* human

Avoid:

* superheroes
* chosen ones
* flawless role models

# Generation Rules

Every prompt should begin with:

```text
Pancakes Canon Character:
[character name]

Maintain exact character continuity.
Use canonical colors.
Use canonical silhouette.
Do not substitute another character.
```

---

# Character Lineup Requirement

Before generating large batches:

1. Generate a lineup sheet.
2. Approve it.
3. Treat it as source of truth.
4. Use the lineup sheet as image reference for future generations.

This is more important than textual descriptions.

---

# Visual Diversity Rule

Characters should differ in:

* age
* life stage
* occupation
* household structure
* presentation
* worldview

rather than merely ethnicity.

The goal is flourishing coverage, not demographic tokenization.
