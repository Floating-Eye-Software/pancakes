# Pancakes User Persona Deck

## Purpose

The Pancakes User Persona Deck provides a recurring cast of representative users used throughout the Pancakes, Pitchfork, Red Witch, and broader humane life computing ecosystem.

These personas are not marketing demographics.

They are not customer acquisition targets.

They are not intended to represent all users.

Instead, they serve as recurring design participants who help evaluate whether the ecosystem supports human flourishing across diverse life situations, responsibilities, capabilities, and values.

The personas represent different perspectives on:

* ownership
* care
* community
* privacy
* meaning
* continuity
* competence
* everyday work
* wisdom
* learning

Together they form a small social world through which design decisions can be explored.

---

# Design Philosophy

The personas exist to answer questions such as:

* Who benefits?
* Who is burdened?
* Who is excluded?
* Who gains agency?
* Who loses autonomy?
* What becomes easier?
* What becomes more meaningful?
* What becomes more durable?

The personas should not be treated as stereotypes.

They are recurring individuals with lives, relationships, strengths, limitations, and histories.

Each persona embodies particular flourishing concerns while remaining a complete human being.

# 1. Boudewijn

## Core Theme

Ownership and Agency

## Archetype

The Builder

## Age

31

## Background

Mechanical engineer and lifelong tinkerer.

Boudewijn enjoys understanding how things work and prefers tools he can inspect, repair, and trust.

He is technically curious but not an extreme self-hosting specialist. He represents ordinary technically literate users rather than infrastructure experts.

He lives with Efua.

## Represents

* ownership
* autonomy
* self-reliance
* practical understanding
* user sovereignty

## Key Questions

* Can I understand this?
* Can I trust this?
* Can I repair this?
* Can I export my data?
* Can I leave if I choose?

## Flourishing Domains

* Agency
* Competence
* Stewardship

# Relationships

The personas form a recurring social world.

Examples include:

* Boudewijn and Efua as a household.
* Fatima and Miriam as community stewards from different generations.
* Riley and Luna exploring privacy, identity, and meaning.
* Priya and Mateo balancing preservation and practicality.
* Samira grounding discussions in everyday work realities.
* Jun reminding the ecosystem that future generations matter.

These relationships provide opportunities to explore tensions, tradeoffs, and complementary perspectives.

---

# Summary

The Pancakes Persona Deck exists to ensure that the ecosystem remains:

* humane without being paternalistic,
* private without being isolating,
* communal without being coercive,
* meaningful without becoming dogmatic,
* practical without becoming reductive,
* and durable without becoming rigid.

The personas represent recurring voices that should always be present when designing humane life computing systems.
