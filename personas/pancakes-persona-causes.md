# Pancakes Persona Causes

## Purpose

This document uses the Pancakes playlist and the Human Flourishing Framework to develop the motivations and priorities of the recurring user personas.

Some causes originate from songs in the Pancakes playlist. Others arise from the broader commitments of humane life computing and may not yet have a dedicated song.

The causes do not change a persona's canonical identity or make them a mentor, guide, or subject-matter expert. They identify issues that have become personally important to the persona and help explain what they notice, protect, and prioritize during design discussions.

The assignments are interpretive uses of the songs within Pancakes. They are not claims about the artists' intent, endorsements by the artists, or complete accounts of the songs' meanings.

## How to Use the Assignments

The assigned persona is the cause's primary steward in the persona deck. Other personas may share the concern, disagree about its implications, or bring a different perspective to it.

Use these causes to ask:

* why a persona cares about a design decision,
* what human consequence they notice first,
* what outcome they would defend when priorities conflict,
* which other personas they would seek out as collaborators.

Do not use the assignments to reduce a persona to a single issue, infer a personal history, or treat a song as evidence of biography.

# Additional Causes Without Dedicated Songs

## Community Vitality and Mutual Support

**Primary Steward:** Fatima

Communities flourish through trust, reciprocity, participation, and mutual aid.

This cause asks whether systems make it easier for people to:

* help one another,
* organize together,
* share responsibility,
* govern collectively,
* remain resilient during hardship.

---

## Capability and Mastery

**Primary Steward:** Mateo

**Supporting Advocates:** Boudewijn, Jun

People flourish when they can learn, become capable, and exercise practical competence.

This cause asks:

* Can beginners participate?
* Are skills teachable?
* Does this build competence?
* Does this reduce unnecessary dependence?

---

## Leisure, Play, and Delight

**Primary Steward:** Luna

**Supporting Advocate:** Jun

Human flourishing includes enjoyment, creativity, curiosity, and harmless pleasures that need no productivity justification.

This cause protects:

* play,
* beauty,
* creativity,
* rest,
* delight,
* spaces that do not demand measurable outcomes.

---

## Human Mystery and Non-Optimization

**Primary Steward:** Luna

**Supporting Advocate:** Riley

People retain the right to ambiguity, symbolism, incomplete understanding, and forms of meaning that resist measurement.

This cause asks:

* Must everything be classified?
* Must everything be scored?
* Can people remain partially unknowable?

---

## Maintenance and Continuity

**Primary Steward:** Priya

**Supporting Advocates:** Miriam, Boudewijn

Flourishing depends upon people who preserve knowledge, repair systems, and care for institutions across time.

This cause asks:

* Who maintains this?
* Who repairs it?
* Will it still work decades from now?
* Are maintainers supported?

# Developed Persona Priorities

## Boudewijn: Capability, Contestability, and Exit

Boudewijn believes people should be able to understand, inspect, question, repair, and leave the systems they use.

He prioritizes:

* explanation,
* repair,
* interoperability,
* export,
* inspectability,
* user sovereignty,
* credible exit paths.

---

## Efua: Justice in Care and Reciprocity

Efua cares about care work being noticed, shared fairly, and supported without losing its relational meaning.

She prioritizes:

* shared responsibility,
* reciprocal care,
* hospitality,
* low-burden coordination,
* relationship maintenance,
* privacy for intimate household life.

---

## Fatima: Community and Collective Power

Fatima believes people flourish together.

She prioritizes:

* democratic governance,
* mutual aid,
* community resilience,
* accessible participation,
* cooperation,
* safeguards against concentrated power.

---

## Riley: Safety Without Erasure

Riley cares about the ability to form and express identity without exposure, persecution, or forced permanence.

They prioritize:

* selective disclosure,
* pseudonymity,
* revocable consent,
* safe exit,
* informational self-determination,
* identity correction,
* user-controlled boundaries.

---

## Luna: Meaning Beyond Measurement

Luna protects forms of life that instrumental systems tend to flatten.

She prioritizes:

* plural interpretation,
* expressive freedom,
* reflection,
* cultural context,
* seasonal awareness,
* play,
* mystery,
* non-instrumental life.

---

## Priya: Memory and Continuity

Priya understands preservation as a form of stewardship.

She prioritizes:

* provenance,
* durable records,
* open formats,
* preservation planning,
* maintenance,
* intergenerational understanding,
* support for maintainers.

---

## Mateo: Competence and Resilient Help

Mateo values practical competence and systems that continue working under ordinary conditions.

He prioritizes:

* capability building,
* resilience,
* practical support,
* graceful recovery,
* clear next steps,
* usefulness under real constraints.

---

## Samira: Material Dignity

Samira cares about fair work and economic room to breathe.

She prioritizes:

* affordability,
* time sovereignty,
* low-friction access,
* schedule tolerance,
* material sufficiency,
* usefulness under constrained conditions.

---

## Miriam: Wisdom, Dignity, and Ordinary Flourishing

Miriam cares about remembering what power has done, affirming the dignity that remains when status and possessions are gone, and preserving the conditions that make ordinary life possible.

She evaluates systems according to the future they create and the forms of human life they protect.

She prioritizes:

* institutional accountability,
* testimony,
* historical context,
* wisdom,
* restoration,
* intergenerational learning,
* trustworthy stewardship,
* nourishment,
* rest,
* ordinary pleasures,
* cultural practices that sustain human flourishing.

For Miriam, the final question is simple:

> After all our systems, struggles, and institutions, are people able to live ordinary, dignified, and enjoyable lives?

---

## Jun: Protected Possibility and Learning

Jun cares about learning, curiosity, and the possibility of helping shape a changing world.

He prioritizes:

* learning,
* age-appropriate participation,
* future opportunity,
* reversible choices,
* privacy,
* curiosity,
* exploration,
* hope.

# Relationship to the Persona Deck

The Pancakes User Persona Deck remains the authoritative definition of the personas' identities, themes, backgrounds, and flourishing domains.

This document extends the deck with motivational causes and advocacy priorities.

The Pancakes Playlist remains the authoritative track order and listening reference. Playlist-derived causes should be reviewed whenever the playlist changes.

Causes without dedicated songs should be treated as equally legitimate parts of the flourishing framework and may acquire musical associations in future revisions.
